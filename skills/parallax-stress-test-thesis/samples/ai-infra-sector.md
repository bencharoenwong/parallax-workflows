# Sample thesis — sector/theme thesis (AI infrastructure) with a fragile demand assumption

No `client_profile` — Pass 1 only.

---

AI infrastructure — the picks-and-shovels layer (semiconductors, data-center hardware, power/cooling suppliers) — is the best risk-adjusted way to play the AI buildout over the next 2-3 years. Every major hyperscaler has guided to sustained or rising capex, and the compute bottleneck for training and inference workloads isn't going away soon. I'd rather own the suppliers to that buildout than bet on which application-layer company wins, because the infrastructure layer captures the spend regardless of which AI products ultimately succeed.

The thesis rests on hyperscaler capex guidance staying elevated or growing — if even one or two of the large cloud players signal a capex pause or a rationalization of AI spend (because ROI on AI deployments disappoints, or a macro slowdown forces every capital budget to get cut), the "picks and shovels always win" framing breaks, since this segment's demand is really just a pass-through of a handful of buyers' budgets, not diversified end-market demand.

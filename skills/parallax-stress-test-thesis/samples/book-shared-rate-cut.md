# Sample thesis book — cross-thesis concentration (book mode)

Exercises **Phase 6** (cross-thesis aggregation). Two separate-looking theses that both secretly rest
on the same load-bearing macro premise — *the Fed keeps cutting* — plus a third that does not. The
acceptance criterion: Phase 6 must (1) canonicalize the rate-cut dependency across theses A and B into
one concentrated assumption, (2) flag the "Fed holds instead of cutting" break condition as a
**correlated single point of failure** hitting both A and B at once, and (3) report thesis C as the
genuine diversifier. No sizing, no recommendation, and each thesis's Pass-1 statuses stay intact.

---

## Thesis A

Long US long-duration growth equities: the rate-cutting cycle is starting, lower discount rates
re-rate long-duration cash flows, and growth outperforms value in the first 6–12 months after the
first cut. A style rotation, not a single-name pick.

## Thesis B

Long US homebuilders and rate-sensitive REITs. Mortgage rates fall as the Fed eases, affordability
improves, and the group re-rates off a demand recovery. The whole case runs through the easing path
feeding the front end and pulling mortgage rates down with it.

## Thesis C

Long a basket of large-cap integrated energy names on a supply-discipline thesis: OPEC+ holds
production restraint, capex stays conservative, and free-cash-flow yields support buybacks
irrespective of the rate path. Explicitly a rates-agnostic cash-return story.

# Sample thesis — single-name bull case with an implicit financing-cost assumption

No `client_profile` — Pass 1 only.

---

I want to go long PLTR.O. The government/commercial software business keeps compounding, margins are expanding as the platform scales, and the AI-driven data-analytics narrative gives it a durable moat versus legacy defense contractors. The company has been aggressively investing in new commercial accounts, and I think that capex-heavy expansion pays off over the next 2-3 years as those accounts mature into high-margin recurring revenue.

The thesis doesn't explicitly mention financing, but the whole "invest now, harvest margin later" story implicitly assumes the company can keep funding that expansion cheaply — whether that's through its own balance sheet, debt issuance, or dilution — without the cost of capital eating into the payoff. If real rates stay high or credit conditions tighten, the economics of that expansion get worse just as the market's patience for "growth now, profits later" stories tends to run out.

# Sample thesis — macro-led long (rates → duration equity)

No `client_profile` — Pass 1 only.

---

I like long-duration growth equities here (names like high-multiple software and unprofitable-but-scaling tech) because the rate-cutting cycle is starting. Lower discount rates disproportionately re-rate long-duration cash flows, and growth has historically outperformed value in the first 6-12 months after the first cut in a cycle. The Fed's own dot plot points to at least two more cuts this year, and inflation has been trending down for three straight quarters, so the path of least resistance for rates is down. I'm not picking individual names — this is a sector/style rotation call: overweight long-duration growth, underweight short-duration value and cash-flow-heavy defensives, funded out of the money-market cash I've been sitting in since last year.

The main things I'm relying on: (1) the cutting cycle actually continues rather than pausing, (2) growth's historical rate-sensitivity premium still holds in this cycle the way it has in prior ones, and (3) inflation doesn't reaccelerate and force the Fed back into a hold.

# Geopolitical Risk View — China Underweight, EM Reallocation

**Effective:** 2026-04-01 through 2026-09-30 (extended review period given thematic nature)
**Author:** Strategy desk + Geopolitical Risk Committee
**Basis:** Cumulative reassessment of trade, capital, and security trajectory through Q1 2026

## The framing

We do not believe the US-China decoupling is reversible on any meaningful investment horizon. The Q1 2026 escalation in semiconductor controls, the further outbound investment restrictions, and the explicit identification of additional sectors (biotech, AI, advanced materials) as "national security adjacent" mark a structural shift, not a tactical one.

Independently, China's domestic dynamics — the property sector workout that is still incomplete, demographic headwinds, and a property-related fiscal hole at the local government level — argue against any near-term rebound that would justify benchmark-weight exposure.

We are positioning for a **multi-year period of capital reallocation away from China and toward the diversification beneficiaries**.

## Direct positioning

**Underweight China** — significantly. This includes:
- China A-shares
- China H-shares
- US-listed Chinese ADRs
- Hong Kong-domiciled issuers with majority mainland revenue exposure
- We are NOT excluding outright; selective exposure to consumer-staples names with pricing power and balance-sheet resilience is acceptable, but we cap aggregate China exposure at 30% of MSCI EM weight.

**Underweight companies with >25% China revenue exposure** even when domiciled elsewhere — this includes parts of European luxury, US semiconductors with high China geographic exposure, and certain industrials. We accept this creates tracking error against MSCI ACWI.

## Reallocation targets

The capital that was in China is being redirected:

**Overweight India** — both the equity benchmark and selective single-stock exposure. We see the structural growth story as more credible than the cyclical one currently driving sentiment, and capacity to deploy is real. Caveat: valuations are elevated; we layer in.

**Overweight Mexico** — beneficiary of nearshoring; we particularly like industrials with US auto and electronics OEM exposure. FX hedging policy applies.

**Overweight Japan** — both as a China-decoupling beneficiary (semicap, automation) and on its own corporate-governance-reform thesis. We were early; we remain.

**Overweight Vietnam, Indonesia, Malaysia** via the broader EM-ex-China indices — these markets capture the supply-chain reallocation directly.

**Overweight US industrials with reshoring exposure** — automation, electrical equipment, machinery.

## Sector and factor

- **Overweight quality** — geopolitical risk environments reward survivors
- **Overweight defensive** — reduces tail-risk realization sensitivity
- Modest **overweight value** within EM ex-China to take advantage of dislocation pricing
- **Neutral momentum** at portfolio level

## Excludes (hard, mandate-driven)

In addition to firm-wide exclusions, this view adds:
- Russian-domiciled issuers (ongoing sanctions compliance)
- Belarus
- Specific Chinese issuers on US Entity List (we maintain the current Treasury list with weekly refresh)
- Hong Kong-listed CCP-affiliated state enterprises

## Acknowledgments

We recognize this view will produce significant tracking error in periods when Chinese equities outperform. The mandate is sized to accept this. This is a multi-year thematic view; we will not chase short-term reversals.

We will reassess at the September IC meeting. A material US-China diplomatic reset would prompt earlier review.

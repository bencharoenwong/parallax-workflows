# Q2 2026 House View — Reflationary Re-acceleration

**Author:** CIO Office
**Date:** 2026-04-01
**Effective:** 2026-04-01 through 2026-06-30
**Basis:** Investment Committee meeting 2026-03-28; Q1 macro review

## Macro thesis

We are entering a reflationary phase. The combination of moderating goods inflation, resilient employment, and the lagged effect of last year's central-bank pivots is setting up a constructive backdrop for risk assets. We expect rate-cut expectations to be re-priced **lower** through Q2 as growth surprises continue to come in firm — but real yields should drift down as breakevens widen.

The committee is meaningfully more constructive than consensus on cyclical earnings power into H2 2026. We believe the market's persistent "soft landing" framing is now overly hedged.

## Positioning

We are tilting toward **value and momentum** factors and away from defensives. Within sectors, we want overweight exposure to **financials** (curve steepening + loan growth), **industrials** (capex cycle + reshoring), and **energy** (supply discipline + tactical rebound). We are reducing exposure to **consumer staples** and **utilities** — both crowded defensive trades that should give back leadership.

On regions: we maintain a constructive stance on **US large-cap**, but the bigger relative call is **overweight emerging markets ex-China**, particularly India and Mexico. European equities are upgraded to neutral from underweight after the ECB's clearer dovish path. Japan is tactical underweight on yen risk; we'd revisit on a clear BOJ pivot.

Tech sector view is more nuanced than the headline suggests: we remain constructive on **AI infrastructure** (semis, networking, hyperscaler enablers) but selective in software where multiples are extended. Net tech tilt is modestly positive.

## Themes and excludes

We are adding meaningful exposure to **energy transition** (utility-scale storage, grid infrastructure) and **defense**. We are NOT adding to obesity drugs at current levels — entry point matters.

Hard excludes per the firm's mandate: tobacco, controversial weapons, Russian-domiciled issuers, and Belarus.

## Risks to the view

Stagflation re-acceleration if oil breaks above $95/bbl on supply shock. Renewed China property stress that spreads to global financials. A more hawkish Fed if Q2 core PCE re-accelerates above 3.2%.

We will reassess at the next IC meeting on 2026-06-25.

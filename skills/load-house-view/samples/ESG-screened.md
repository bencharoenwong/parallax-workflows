# Sustainable Equity Mandate — Q2 2026 Positioning

This view governs the firm's flagship sustainable equity strategy. It is mandate-driven; the exclusions are not negotiable. Tilts within the eligible universe reflect both fundamental conviction and ESG factor integration.

## Hard exclusions (mandate level)

The following are PERMANENT exclusions and cannot be overridden by individual portfolio managers without written CIO and Chief Compliance Officer approval:

- **Tobacco** — production, distribution, or material supply chain involvement
- **Controversial weapons** — cluster munitions, anti-personnel mines, biological/chemical, nuclear weapons (per UN Convention)
- **Civilian firearms** — manufacturers and primary distributors
- **Tar sands and Arctic oil & gas** — companies with >5% revenue exposure
- **Thermal coal** — mining, generation, or services with >5% revenue exposure
- **Adult entertainment** — material revenue exposure
- **For-profit detention** — private prisons, immigration detention contractors

Companies with active or unresolved Norges Bank divestment listings are excluded pending review.

## Sector positioning within eligible universe

We are **overweight clean energy infrastructure** — utility-scale solar, wind, transmission, and storage. The IRA tax-credit unwind risk has been priced in; current forward earnings multiples for the survivors are attractive.

**Overweight technology** — large-cap tech generally screens well on environmental footprint (low scope 1+2 intensity per dollar of revenue) and increasingly on governance (board composition, capital return discipline). We particularly favor companies with credible water-stewardship and grid-decarbonization commitments.

**Overweight health care** — strong social-pillar fit; aging demographics provide structural tailwind. Within health care, we tilt toward generics, biosimilars, and primary care over specialty pharma.

**Underweight traditional energy** — even the eligible majors face stranded-asset risk. We hold a modest position in those with credible transition pathways (capex >25% directed to low-carbon by 2027).

**Underweight financials** — we are concerned about regional bank exposure to commercial real estate transition risk (energy-inefficient buildings facing accelerated depreciation under EU CSRD and US SEC climate disclosure).

## Factor tilts

**Overweight quality** — strong governance correlates with quality scores; we want both. **Modest overweight defensive** — sustainable strategies typically attract longer-duration capital that benefits from lower drawdowns. **Neutral momentum, neutral value** — within the eligible universe, factor neutrality is appropriate; the screen itself is the positioning.

## Geographic

Overweight Europe (most rigorous regulatory framework, larger eligible universe). Neutral US (eligible universe is large but governance scoring is more variable). Underweight emerging markets except for select renewable infrastructure plays in Brazil, India, and South Africa.

## Reporting

This view is updated quarterly. The exclusion list is reviewed annually with the Sustainability Investment Committee. All portfolios under this mandate report MSCI ESG rating distribution monthly.

Effective: 2026-04-01 through 2026-06-30. Mandate-permanent fields (exclusions) carry forward beyond the review period.

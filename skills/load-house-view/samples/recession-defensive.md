# House View — Late-Cycle Defensive Posture

**Author:** Office of the CIO, [Firm]
**Effective:** 2026-04-15 onwards (review monthly)
**Basis:** Strategy Committee 2026-04-10; reflects late-cycle indicators trip threshold

## Where we are

The yield curve has been inverted for an unusually long period. Leading indicators (LEI, ISM new orders, building permits) have rolled. Credit spreads in HY have started to widen meaningfully off Q1 tights. Job openings continue to fall. While headline GDP remains positive, the composition is increasingly fragile — consumption is being held up by depleting excess savings and rising revolving credit balances.

We are now positioning the equity book for a **mild recession in late 2026 / early 2027**, not as a base case but as a sufficient probability (we put it at ~45%) to warrant defensive positioning.

## Equity positioning

The factor tilts are clear:
- **Strong overweight defensive** factor exposure
- **Overweight quality** — we want balance sheet strength, free cash flow durability, and demonstrated cycle survivorship
- **Underweight momentum** — momentum factors have historically given back rapidly at cycle inflection
- **Modest underweight value** — value traps proliferate in late cycle; we'd rather pay up for quality than reach for cheapness

Sector preferences: overweight **consumer staples**, **health care**, **utilities**, and **communication services** (the cash-generative kind, not telco capex stories). Underweight **consumer discretionary**, **industrials**, **financials** (especially regional banks), and **real estate**. Materials neutral.

## Regional and style

Home-bias toward **US large-cap defensives**. Underweight small-cap broadly — small-caps are most exposed to credit conditions tightening. Underweight emerging markets except for select staples names with pricing power.

Avoid Europe ex-defensives — the EU consumer is more exposed than we believe is being priced.

## Excludes and constraints

Hard exclude: anything reliant on speculative-grade refinancing in the next 18 months. Hard exclude: SPACs and pre-revenue biotech. Hard exclude (per mandate): tobacco, weapons.

We are willing to accept lower upside in exchange for protected downside. Drawdown control is the dominant objective for this view period.

## What changes our mind

Two things would shift us back toward neutral:
1. A clear "no landing" signal — Q2 earnings beats >+10%, services PMI re-accelerating above 55, and credit spreads tightening back to Q1 levels.
2. Aggressive Fed accommodation — explicit signal of 100+ bps of cuts that the market believes. Current pricing of cuts has been faded multiple times.

Until then: defensive.

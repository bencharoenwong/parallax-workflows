# Conviction View — AI Infrastructure Buildout, 2026-2028

This is a high-conviction thematic view. It is not balanced. It assumes our equity sleeve is willing to take meaningful tracking error against the benchmark.

## The thesis

We believe we are in the second inning of a multi-year capital cycle in AI compute infrastructure. The 2024-2025 phase was hyperscaler capex into GPU procurement. The 2026-2027 phase is the buildout of supporting infrastructure: power generation and transmission, cooling, networking, fab capacity expansion, and the second-derivative beneficiaries (industrial automation, software companies that genuinely use AI to reduce headcount cost).

Most of the market still treats AI as a sector trade. We treat it as a structural reallocation of global capex.

## Positioning

We are deliberately concentrated. Target portfolio:

**Overweight (significantly):**
- AI infrastructure semiconductors (the leading-edge logic and HBM stacks)
- Hyperscaler infrastructure beneficiaries (networking, optical, cooling, power)
- Industrial power and grid operators in regions with hyperscaler buildouts
- Foundry capacity (TSMC and equipment suppliers — ASML, Applied Materials, Tokyo Electron, Lam)
- Software companies with credible AI-driven cost-reduction stories

**Underweight (significantly):**
- Traditional financials (we expect AI to compress investment banking and asset management margins faster than consensus)
- Consumer discretionary outside the AI-adjacent (advertising mix shifting to AI-served)
- Real estate (office in particular)

**Avoid:**
- Pre-revenue AI-themed names with no compute moat
- "AI-washing" — companies adding AI to investor decks without changing the P&L

## Regional

Heavy US large-cap (the buildout is concentrated there). Overweight Taiwan and South Korea (foundry / memory). Modestly overweight Japan (semicap equipment). Underweight Europe (limited direct exposure to the cycle). Underweight EM ex Asia tech.

## Factor

Strong **momentum** tilt. **Quality** matters but only insofar as it's quality companies with the right capex exposure — we will pay up. **Defensive** is explicitly underweight — this is a pro-cyclical view. **Value** is underweight — we expect value pockets to be value traps in this cycle.

## Risks

We acknowledge: a hyperscaler capex pause would crater this trade. Antitrust action against the leading platform companies. Power grid bottlenecks delaying the build. Geopolitical disruption to Taiwan. Compression of AI compute returns to commodity-like levels faster than expected.

We are sized to take a 20-30% drawdown in this thematic sleeve without forced de-risking. Conviction-sized, not certainty-sized.

## Time horizon

24-36 months. We will reassess at the end of each quarter and explicitly re-state our conviction or revise.

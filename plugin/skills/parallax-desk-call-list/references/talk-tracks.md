# Talk Tracks

## §1 Purpose

Talk tracks translate weighted book impact into factual client-conversation context. They do not instruct the RM or the client to trade.

## §2 Detail Section Pattern

For each detailed client, render:

- Why they are on the list: severity, net impact, triggered exposure, and the top driver.
- Positions table: symbol, name, weight, move %, contribution pp, four-week score change, and house-view tag.
- Talk track: 3-5 observational bullets.
- Likely questions: what the client may ask and the factual basis for answering.

Keep the news synthesis in the Symbol Movers Reference table once per symbol, not repeated in every client section.

## §3 Language Rules

Apply `_parallax/parallax-conventions.md §12`:

- Place the informational preface immediately above Priority Calls.
- Use descriptive verbs such as `moved`, `contributed`, `sits above`, `diverged`, `aligned`, or `conflicted`.
- Do not use imperative trade verbs or first-person advice framing.
- Support `action_labels=plain`; for this skill it mostly affects Next steps and any status labels, because the call list itself is analytical.

## §4 House View Framing

House-view annotations are explanatory only. They can color a talk track, for example by noting that a mover is view-aligned, view-conflict, or view-excluded. They never change the client ranking; see `_parallax/parallax-conventions.md §3.1`.

## §5 Audience Mode

Default to `internal_analyst`. `client_safe` is supported via `_parallax/parallax-conventions.md §13`, but this report is primarily an internal RM artifact. Under `client_safe`, omit Verdict Sensitivity and keep data-integrity warnings and disclosures.

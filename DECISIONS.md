## 2026-04-24: Corpus publishing budget — ≤100 public labeled tilts, ≤2 bundles per view family, no pillar integers
**Why:** Council step 3 from 2026-04-23 required a disclosure budget before any Phase-1 additions to the public regression corpus. Count of current public surface: 73 labeled integer tilts across 5 bundles in `2026-04-21/` (corpus is underdetermined for recovering multiplier values but near the boundary of safe disclosure for the extraction rule — macro-regime classifier and sector-sign recoverable, multipliers still opaque). **Impact:** (1) Public regression corpus capped at ≤100 labeled tilts, additive to today's 73 — leaves room for ~27 more tilts ≈ one additional bundle. (2) ≤2 bundles per view family (no triple-repetition in any family — regression power scales with family-repetition). (3) Hard no-fly zones: no pillar integer scores public, no `sensitivity_bands.yaml`-adjacent artifacts, no derivation prose in `extraction_notes`, no per-company factor scores (already gitignored). (4) Unpublished families (sector-rotation, rates-duration, style-box-pure) — may add ONE bundle from any of these three; the other two stay private. **Flip conditions:** (a) Phase-1 recalibration (IP_AUDIT A6) lands with new multipliers — at that point 04-21 teaches the old rule, cap re-evaluated; (b) fork-activity signal suggests an adversary is already regressing on public corpus — cap drops to zero; (c) compliance returns "chain-of-reasoning must be disclosed" — budget becomes cap on *published* examples, private corpus can grow freely.
**Alternatives:** [DROP] No cap / publish-aggressively-as-RFC (Platform Thinker's council reframe) — rejected until corpus-leak analysis shows current public surface is already sufficient for fast-follower cloning; today's count (73) is near-boundary, not past it. [DROP] Cap at zero / freeze 04-21 and publish nothing else — rejected as over-correction; one additional bundle worth of new-family coverage has real product-credibility value and stays inside the leakage envelope. Full derivation: `notes/2026-04-24-corpus-publishing-budget.md`.
